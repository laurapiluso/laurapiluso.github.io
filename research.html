Why Do Workers Leave Firms? Worker Separations, Firm Effects, and Social Incentives

PhD research project

This project studies why workers leave firms by focusing on the final stage of the employment relationship, distinguishing between voluntary separations, namely quits, and involuntary separations, namely layoffs. The empirical part uses COB administrative data for Emilia-Romagna and extends an AKM framework to estimate the probability of quits and layoffs, decomposing their variability into worker effects, firm effects, and a residual component. Building on these results, the theoretical part models the microeconomic mechanism underlying quits through a dynamic Public Good Game with exit, where the public good captures a component of workplace quality. The model’s implications are intended to be tested through a laboratory experiment.

Quantitative Mapping of Italy’s Twin Transition: A Multidimensional Territorial Analysis

With Francesco Aiello, Simone Baricca, and Valeria Pupo

Work in progress

This project develops a multidimensional territorial index to map technological, environmental, and socio-economic dimensions of the twin transition across Italian municipalities.
