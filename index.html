PhD Candidate in Labour, Development and Innovation

Marco Biagi Department of Economics - University of Modena and Reggio Emilia

Fondazione Marco Biagi

I am a PhD Candidate in Labour, Development and Innovation at the Marco Biagi Department of Economics, University of Modena and Reggio Emilia and Fondazione Marco Biagi.

My research interests are in labour economics, applied microeconometrics, and behavioural microeconomics. My current research studies why workers leave firms, focusing on voluntary and involuntary separations, firm effects, worker heterogeneity, and the microeconomic mechanisms underlying quits.

I work mainly with Italian administrative data and matched employer–employee data, combining applied econometric analysis with theoretical modelling.
